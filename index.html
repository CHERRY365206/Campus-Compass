Academic Life

Everything you need to know about B.Tech at Amrita Amaravati.

B.Tech Branches Available

💻 Computer Science & Engineering
🤖 CSE (Artificial Intelligence)
📊 AI & Data Science
📡 Computer & Communication

Expect a curriculum that blends strong fundamentals with cutting-edge tech. Labs are intensive here, so make sure you take your practicals seriously—they carry significant weightage!

Exam Pattern

  • Continuous Assessments (50%): Trust us, don't take these lightly! Class tests, assignments, and projects make up half your grade.
  • Mid Semester Exam (20%): The first major milestone of the semester.
  • End Semester Exam (30%): The final hurdle.
  • Pro Tip: Consistent effort in your CIAs saves you massive stress during the final exam week.

Attendance Rules 🚨

We can't stress this enough: attendance is strictly enforced. You MUST keep it above 75%-80%. If you drop below, you'll get an 'FA' (Failed due to Attendance) and won't be allowed to write the final exam. Save your bunks for when you actually need them!

Tips to maintain a good CGPA from Day 1

Don't ignore CIAs

🤝

Peer learning is the best way to tackle tough CSE concepts.

💻

Code Daily

Don't wait for lab days to write code. Consistency is key.

Campus & Hostel Life

Surviving and thriving in your new home away from home.

First Month at College Timeline

Week 1: Orientation & Onboarding

Familiarize yourself with the campus layout, get your ID card set up, and attend the welcoming sessions. It's overwhelming but fun!

Week 2: Classes Begin

Syllabus drops. You'll meet your professors and get access to AUMS (Amrita University Management System).

Week 3: Club Inductions

The most exciting week! Tech and cultural clubs start their recruitment drives. Keep an eye out for posters.

Week 4: First Periodicals Approach

Reality check. The first set of CIA exams will be announced. Time to start visiting the library.

The Fresher's Survival Kit

Mistakes to avoid and hacks to survive your first year.

✅ The Do's

  • Make the library your second home (plus, the AC is great!).
  • Build a good rapport with your professors—they're incredibly helpful if you show genuine interest.
  • Join at least one tech and one cultural club to keep things balanced.
  • Back up your laptop files to the cloud weekly. Trust us on this one.

❌ The Don'ts

  • Don't even try to proxy for attendance; it rarely works and isn't worth the risk.
  • Try not to leave assignments until 11:59 PM.
  • Don't get into bad terms with hostel wardens. Be polite.
  • And seriously, avoid living entirely on instant noodles—go eat at the mess!
